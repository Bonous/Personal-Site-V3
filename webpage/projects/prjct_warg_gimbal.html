WARG GoPro Gimbal


Background

In mid-May, future University of Waterloo students were invited to join the Waterloo Aerial Robotics Group. Before joining a sub-team members are required to complete a Bootcamp. For joining the mechanical sub-team you are required to design a gimbal in SolidWorks for a GoPro Hero 3. This project features the gimbal I designed that got me admitted to the sub-team. The gimbal had the following requirements for the bootcamp: A minimum of 120° of freedom in the pitch axis and 90° in the roll axis. A small mounting footprint (at most 60 mm x 60 mm square). A total weight (including the camera) of 300 g or less, and must be easily manufacturable.


My Design

The gimbal features primarily bent aluminum sheet metal parts along with a 3d printed PLA GoPro case. It uses 2 off the shelf brushless motors along with a brushless gimbal controller board and a mpu6050 IMU. The control board is mounted between 2 3d printed TPU spacers to reduce vibrations. My design met all the required specifications for the bootcamp.
